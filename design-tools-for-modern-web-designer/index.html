Design Tools for Modern Web Designer

It’s 2015, are you still using Photoshop for website mock up? Time to make your life easier and speed up development with the shiny new tools covered in this blog post.

1. Sketch 3

If you have not heard of Sketch, you should seriously check it out and give it a try. It’s lightweight, fast, and intuitive, especially compare to something like Photoshop. Unlike Photoshop, which is primarily built for photo manipulations, Sketch is built from the ground up for UI design, perfect for different screen sizes. Aside from art boards like those you find in Illustrator, you also have different pages, which could contain multiple art boards with dimensions of your choice. Moreover, you can unleash your creativity on its unlimited canvas. Many designers in Google, Apple, and Facebook speak highly of the app. The only downside is it’s only available on Mac OS X. The cost is $99 (usd) with a 50% off student discount. There are also tons of free resources on the web such as those from sketchappsources.com and a third party plugin system.

Out of all the resources listed on this post, this is the one you should get if you could only pick one.

2. Framer.js

Sketch is for static UI design. When it comes to interactive animations, Framer is the new king.

Framer is an effective prototyping app for Mac, that allows designers to experiment with animations and interactions in CoffeeScript. Yes, you do need to
know a bit of programming, but once you do, nothing beats its prowess.

Moreover, it integrates with Sketch, so you could design and prototype your app in never before speed. The price is same as Sketch where $99 gives you a full copy with the possible 50% off student discount.

3. Invision

Another app that is adopted by a lot of big companies such as Twitter and Evernote. What it does is you can import your design with different pages in formats like sketch or psd. After drawing rectangle areas for buttons, say on a navigation menu, you could link different pages together by clicking that button, simulating a real application. No longer you have to present the half finished html product to your client; let them be happy with the user experience first before starting to code. It is a huge time saver that is well worth the money if you have ongoing projects.

Unlike the previous two apps, this one follows a subscription model with a free tier.

Conclusion

This about sums it up the essential apps for developing the modern web. There are also tools like webflow where your design automatically turns into html and css, but it is less flexible due to its pricing model.
